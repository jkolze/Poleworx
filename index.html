Fireman

Starting Position: Begin by standing facing the pole with your inside arm (the arm closest to the pole) raised above your head and gripping the pole with an overhand grip. Your outside arm (the arm farthest from the pole) should be extended straight out to the side for balance. Initiating the Spin: Step your inside leg forward and slightly to the side of the pole. Push off with your outside leg and use your inside arm to pull yourself around the pole, initiating the spin. Your body should be slightly tilted back to help create momentum. Bringing Legs Together: As you begin to spin, bring your legs together and extend them straight out in front of you. Keep your toes pointed and legs parallel to the ground.

  • On the test? Yes

Martini

On the ground, stand next to the pole. Hook the inside leg as high as you can.
Use the outside hand to grab the pole above the hooked knee.
Bring the outside leg up while keeping it straight.

  • On the test? Yes

Pinwheel Spin

Starting next to the pole, bring your inside hand up high and your outside hand across your chest in a half-bracket grip. You will be utilizing a Push â€“ Pull method with your arms. Step forward with your inside leg. Swing your outside leg wide around your body to create momentum. Bring both legs up and bend the knees, bringing them away from the pole. Your front leg will be in front, and your back leg will be pushed out to the side.

  • On the test? Yes

Chair

As you begin to spin, bend your outside knee and bring it up towards your chest, creating a "chair" shape with your body. Keep your inside leg extended straight out in front of you or slightly to the side.

  • On the test? Yes

Back Hook Spin

Starting Position: Begin by standing facing the pole with your inside arm raised above your head and your outside arm extended straight out to the side for balance. Your inside leg should be slightly bent and positioned in front of the pole, while your outside leg is extended straight behind you.
Gripping the Pole: Reach your inside hand up and over the pole, gripping it with an overhand grip. Your thumb should be pointing upwards, and your fingers wrapped securely around the pole.
Initiating the Spin: Push off with your outside foot and use your inside arm to pull yourself around the pole, initiating the spin. Your body should be slightly tilted back to help create momentum, and your outside arm can act as a guide as you rotate.
Creating the Back Hook: As you spin, bend your outside leg and hook it behind the pole, creating a "hook" shape with your leg. Your knee should be bent at a 90-degree angle, and your foot should be flexed to grip the pole securely.

  • On the test? Yes

Pole Sit

Starting Position: Begin by standing facing the pole with both hands gripping the pole above your head, arms slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Your inside arm (the arm closest to the pole) should be higher than your outside arm, creating an asymmetrical grip.
Gripping the Pole: Reach your hands up and over the pole, gripping it with an overhand grip. Your thumbs should be pointing upwards, and your fingers wrapped securely around the pole.
Initiating the Climb: Use your arms to pull yourself up the pole, engaging your core and lifting your legs off the ground. Your knees should be bent, and your shins pressing against the pole.
Securing the Pole Sit: Once you have reached a comfortable height, release your hands from the pole and bring your knees up towards your chest. Cross your ankles and hook your feet around the pole, securing yourself in the Pole Sit position.

  • On the test? Yes

Craddle

Starting Position: Begin by standing facing the pole with your inside arm (the arm closest to the pole) raised above your head and your outside arm extended straight out to the side for balance. Your inside leg should be slightly bent and positioned in front of the pole, while your outside leg is extended straight behind you.
Gripping the Pole: Reach your inside hand up and over the pole, gripping it with an overhand grip. Your thumb should be pointing upwards, and your fingers wrapped securely around the pole. Your outside hand can also grip the pole for added stability.
Initiating the Spin: Push off with your outside foot and use your inside arm to pull yourself around the pole, initiating the spin. Your body should be slightly tilted back to help create momentum, and your outside arm can act as a guide as you rotate.
Creating the Cradle Position: As you spin, bend your inside leg and bring it up towards your chest, crossing it over your body and hooking it behind the pole. Your knee should be bent, and your foot should be flexed to grip the pole securely. At the same time, extend your outside leg out to the side or behind you, creating a visually pleasing cradle shape with your body.

Hello Boys

Lower your top hand and keep it bent so it pulls your chest in towards the pole. Tilt your body to the side of the bottom leg. Bring the bottom knee up so that it sits gently on your wrist. Bend the top leg up, extending the top arm to give you more room, and when you feel secure, extend both legs.

Reverse Grab Spin

Starting Position: Begin by standing facing the pole with your inside arm raised above your head and your outside arm extended straight out to the side for balance. Your inside leg should be slightly bent and positioned in front of the pole, while your outside leg is extended straight behind you. Gripping the Pole: Reach your inside hand up and over the pole, gripping it with a reverse grip. In a reverse grip, your palm faces away from you, and your thumb points downwards. Your fingers should be wrapped securely around the pole, and your thumb should be pressed against the pole for stability. Your outside hand can also grip the pole for added support, either in a regular or reverse grip. Initiating the Spin: Push off with your outside foot and use your inside arm to pull yourself around the pole, initiating the spin. Your body should be slightly tilted back to help create momentum, and your outside arm can act as a guide as you rotate. Creating Momentum: As you spin, engage your core muscles and focus on maintaining control. Allow your body to naturally rotate around the pole, keeping your arms engaged and your grip secure. Maintaining the Reverse Grab: Throughout the spin, maintain your grip on the pole with your inside arm in the reverse position. Keep your fingers wrapped around the pole and your thumb pressed against it for stability. Rotating Around the Pole: As you spin, allow your body to rotate around the pole while maintaining the reverse grab grip. Your inside leg can remain bent or slightly extended in front of you for balance, while your outside leg can be extended straight behind you or bent at the knee.

  • On the test? Yes
